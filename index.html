Description

This project involved simulating the urban expansion of Pune City over a 40-year period. Land Use (LU) classification was performed for every five years from 1990 to 2025 using satellite imagery in Google Earth Engine. The MOLUSCE plugin in QGIS was then used to train a Cellular Automata - Artificial Neural Network model. This model simulated the land use for 2025, which was then validated against the actual analyzed data for the same year before simulating a future scenario for 2030.

Area Change Analysis (1990-2025)

Class 1990 (Sqkm) 1990 (%) 2025 (Sqkm) 2025 (%) Change %
Built up 120.4 23.6 193.46 38.14 11.6
Vegetation 87.8 17.2 130.75 25.78 7.3
Water body 8.4 1.6 5.96 1.17 -0.5
Barren Land 293.2 57.5 176.94 34.89 -18.4

Key Findings

  • The predictive model demonstrated decent accuracy, with an percentage of correctness - 77.8% and a Kappa coefficient of 69.7% when validating the 2025 simulation.
  • Based on the results of simulation, built-up in increased from 193.46 Sqkm in 2025 to 215.21 Sqkm in 2030, Vegetation decreased from 130.75 Sqkm to 123.64 Sqkm during the same period.
  • As result of rapid urbanisation, the area of barren land decreased from 176.94 Sqkm in 2025 to 165.18 Sqkm in 2030.

Description

This analysis aimed to identify the most suitable locations for new Ultra-Modern Garbage Transfer Facilities within the Bhopal Municipal Corporation area. Using a multi-criteria evaluation (MCE) approach in QGIS and Google Earth Engine, factors like proximity to roads, distance from water bodies, land use type, and slope were analyzed to generate a final suitability map.

Key Findings

  • Pinpointed specific parcels of land that meet all the necessary criteria for facility placement.
  • Provided a data-driven basis for municipal decision-making on waste management infrastructure.

Description

This project focused on quantifying the changes in Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) within Pune City between 2019 and 2025. The Random Forest classification algorithm in Google Earth Engine was used to classify satellite imagery into categories such as urban, vegetation, water, and barren land. Accuracy assessments were then conducted to validate the classification results.

Change Matrix Explanation

  • 11.3% area of barren land in 2019 became built up in 2025. Proves that city is spatially expanding continuously.
  • 21.2 % of total area was built-up in 2019 and is constant in 2025 as well.
  • Though there is decrease in overall area of vegetation, slight increase of 3.6% area of barren land in 2019 became vegetation in 2025. This is the result of plantation drives by PMC.

Key Findings

  • Between 2019 and 2025, Pune Municipal Corporation witnessed a notable rise in built-up area from 151 to 175.3 sq.km (4.8%), primarily at the expense of vegetation, barren land, and water bodies.
  • The urban expansion is attributed to rapid population growth, IT sector development in Hinjewadi and Kharadi, and ongoing infrastructure projects like Metro Rail and road widening.
  • This shift indicates intensifying urbanization, stressing the need for sustainable land management.

Description

This analysis utilized various spectral indices to study the environmental characteristics of Pune. By processing Landsat and Sentinel satellite data in Google Earth Engine, the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI), Normalized Difference Built-up Index (NDBI), and Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) were calculated to map and quantify vegetation health, urban density, and surface water, respectively.

Key Findings

  • Quantified changes in green cover and urban heat island intensity over time.
  • Mapped the extent of surface water bodies and their seasonal variations.
  • Demonstrated the effectiveness of multi-index analysis for comprehensive environmental monitoring.

Description

Using Census 2011 data, this project conducted a comparative statistical analysis of female literacy and employment across the wards of the Pune Municipal Corporation (PMC) and Pimpri Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC). The analysis employed Linear Regression models to understand correlations and K-Means Clustering, a Machine Learning technique, to identify patterns and group wards with similar socio-economic characteristics.

Key Findings

  • Identified a positive correlation between female literacy rates and female workforce participation in both corporations.
  • Segmented the wards into distinct clusters, revealing spatial patterns of socio-economic development.
  • Provided insights that can inform targeted policy interventions for improving female education and employment opportunities.
